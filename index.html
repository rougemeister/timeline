10-Year Career Development Plan

From Junior Developer to Tech Leader & Academic Contributor

Vision & Purpose

My vision is to build a career that connects professional software engineering, entrepreneurship, and academic research. I believe that modern technology leaders thrive at the intersection of these domains. By developing deep technical skills, pursuing business innovation, and contributing to knowledge-sharing through teaching and writing, I aim to create a meaningful and lasting impact in the tech ecosystem.

Career Journey Timeline

I

Technical Foundation and Integration

Years 1–2

Building a strong foundation in software engineering by mastering Angular, modern web technologies, and full-stack development principles. Currently a Junior Developer under the Graduate Trainee Programme at AmaliTech, focusing on real-world projects, agile methodologies, and beginning technical writing on platforms like Medium and Dev.to.

II

Professional Growth and Community Engagement

Years 3–4

Advancing to mid-level developer with increased responsibilities, mentoring others, and leading smaller teams. Developing leadership and communication skills while increasing involvement in the tech ecosystem through consistent writing, speaking at meetups, contributing to open-source projects, and researching master's degree programs.

III

Senior Engineering and Entrepreneurial Initiatives

Years 5–6

Becoming a senior developer or team lead, driving architecture decisions and mentoring junior engineers. Entrepreneurial ambitions take shape with groundwork for first tech startup. Pursuing a master's degree in Computer Science to deepen technical knowledge and prepare for future PhD program.

IV

Startup Growth and Academic Research

Years 7–8

Taking on strategic and technical leadership roles as startup matures, potentially as CTO. Beginning PhD studies, exploring research areas that align with academic and entrepreneurial interests. Expanding public presence through technical talks, articles, workshops, and academic publications.

V

Industry Leadership and Academic Excellence

Years 9–10

Becoming a recognized leader in the tech industry and PhD graduate. Transitioning into a professorial role while continuing to lead innovative projects. Focus on mentoring future leaders, combining academic theory with practical experience, and expanding influence through thought leadership, teaching, and research.
